The Hair Transplant Process

Hair transplant is a surgical procedure that involves moving hair follicles from a donor area (typically the back or sides of your head where hair is more resistant to balding) to the recipient area (where hair loss has occurred).

Types of Hair Transplant Procedures:

  • **FUE (Follicular Unit Extraction):** Individual hair follicles are extracted directly from the donor area using a micro-punch tool and then implanted into the balding areas. This method is popular for its minimally invasive nature and lack of linear scarring.
  • **FUT (Follicular Unit Transplantation):** A strip of skin containing hair follicles is removed from the donor area, and then individual follicular units are harvested from this strip and transplanted. While it can yield a large number of grafts, it leaves a linear scar. (Note: Mention which one you primarily offer or if both are available, adjust accordingly).

During your consultation at Aviva OP Clinic, Dr. Haripriya will assess your hair loss, discuss your expectations, and recommend the most suitable hair transplant technique for you.

Who Is a Good Candidate for Hair Transplant?

Ideal candidates for hair transplant typically include:

  • Individuals with male or female pattern baldness.
  • Those who have sufficient donor hair supply.
  • People with realistic expectations about the results.
  • Individuals whose hair loss has stabilized.

A thorough consultation with Dr. Haripriya is essential to determine your candidacy and discuss the expected outcomes.
