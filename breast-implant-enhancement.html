What is Breast Implant & Enhancement Surgery?

Breast enhancement, also known as augmentation mammoplasty, involves placing silicone or saline implants beneath the breast tissue or chest muscles to increase breast size, improve shape, or restore volume lost due to weight loss, pregnancy, or aging.

This procedure is highly customizable, allowing you to choose the size, shape, and type of implant that suits your body and aesthetic goals.

Reasons Women Choose Breast Augmentation:

  • To enhance naturally small breasts
  • To restore breast volume after pregnancy or weight loss
  • To correct asymmetry or uneven breast size
  • To improve overall body proportions
  • To boost self-image and confidence

The Procedure at a Glance:

  • Duration: Typically 1–2 hours.
  • Type of anesthesia: General anesthesia is usually administered.
  • Hospital stay: Often a same-day discharge, or sometimes an overnight stay for monitoring.
  • Recovery time: Patients can typically resume daily activities within 1–2 weeks, with complete healing over 4–6 weeks.
  • Scarring: Minimal and strategically hidden (commonly under the breast fold, around the nipple, or in the underarm).
